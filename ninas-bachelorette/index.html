Arrive & Settle In

Fly into Jackson Hole Airport — one of the only airports inside a national park. The Tetons hit you before you even leave the terminal.
Thursday
27

Touch Down in Teton Country

Fly into Jackson Hole Airport (JAC), pick up rental cars, and head to the Teton Cabins. Unpack, crack open something cold, and soak in the mountain views from the porch.

Late afternoon: stroll into downtown Jackson (about 15 min drive). Walk the Town Square with its famous elk antler arches, pop into the shops, and get the lay of the land.

Dinner somewhere easy and celebratory. Snake River Brewing is the move — award-winning craft beer, solid food, and the kind of vibe where 12 girls rolling in feels totally natural. Or try Bin22 for wine and small plates if you want something more intimate.

Home Base

Teton Cabins

Family-owned log cabin retreat on a working ranch with views straight to the Teton Range. Cozy, authentic, and the kind of place that makes you want to sit outside with a blanket and a drink and just stare at the mountains. Cabins sleep 2–6 each. They've been ranching this land since the early 1900s.

Horses, Rodeo & Town

Morning in the saddle, evening at the rodeo, night in cowboy boots. This is the day that sets the tone.
Friday
28

Morning: Saddle Up

Book a 2-hour group trail ride for the morning while it's cool and the light is golden. Most outfitters accommodate large groups and all experience levels. You'll ride through sagebrush meadows with the Tetons as your backdrop — legitimately cinematic.

For 12 riders, book well in advance. A couple of the best options:

Spring Creek Ranch
On-site stables above Jackson. Stunning Teton views. 1–2 hour rides, beginner-friendly.
Mill Iron Ranch
Locally owned. Rides through Hoback/Snake River Canyon area. 2–4 hour options. More rugged terrain.
Teton Horseback Adventures (Swift Creek)
Grand Teton scenic trails. Gentle rides through wildflower meadows. Great for groups.

eve

Rodeo & Night Out

Back to the cabins to clean up, then head into Jackson for the big night. Western chic encouraged — this is the bachelorette night.

Early dinner (6 PM): Eat before the rodeo so you're fueled up.

Glorietta Trattoria
Award-winning Italian. Handmade pasta, wood-fired meats, excellent wine list. Reserve early for 12.
The Whistling Grizzly
Upscale Western. Beautiful space, excellent cocktails, and the kind of menu that makes everyone happy.

8 PM: Jackson Hole Rodeo — bull riding, barrel racing, broncs, and all the Wyoming energy you can handle. Gates open at 6:30 PM if you want to grab beers and get settled early. Tickets at jhrodeo.com. Runs every Friday in August.

After the rodeo (~10 PM):

Million Dollar Cowboy Bar
THE Jackson Hole bar since 1937. Saddle barstools, live music, line dancing. On Town Square. Non-negotiable.
The Rose
Speakeasy-style cocktail bar. Enter through the pink door. Craft cocktails, moody lighting.
Silver Dollar Bar (The Wort Hotel)
2,032 silver dollars inlaid in the bar top. Live music most nights. A Jackson institution.

Saturday, August 29

Yellowstone

The full Lower Loop in one epic day. Geysers, prismatic hot springs, a canyon twice as tall as Niagara, and bison traffic jams. Leave early, pack snacks, bring your camera.

The Route: Lower Loop (Clockwise)

Drive from Jackson through Grand Teton NP into Yellowstone via the South Entrance. Loop clockwise: West Thumb → Old Faithful → Grand Prismatic → Firehole Canyon → Norris Geyser Basin → Grand Canyon of Yellowstone → Yellowstone Lake → back to Jackson. Total: ~10–12 hours including 6 hours of driving and 6 hours exploring.

Download the official NPS map — cell service is basically nonexistent in the park.

Saturday
29

The Full Yellowstone Day

This is the crown jewel. You were out last night, so we're starting at a humane hour — 8 AM. You'll hit slightly more crowds than a dawn start, but you'll actually be conscious for the views. Pack breakfast burritos, lunch, and plenty of water the night before.

Pro tip: Check Old Faithful's next eruption time before you leave Jackson at GeyserTimes.org. Eruptions are every ~90 minutes. Time your arrival to catch one without waiting around.

8:00 AM

Leave Jackson

Coffee in hand, snacks packed. Drive north through Grand Teton National Park to Yellowstone's South Entrance. The Tetons will be lit gold on your left the whole way.

53 miles · ~1 hr 15 min to South Entrance
9:30 AM

West Thumb Geyser Basin

First taste of Yellowstone's thermal magic. A short boardwalk loop on the shores of Yellowstone Lake — bubbling mud pots, the turquoise Abyss Pool, and Fishing Cone. Quick stop, huge payoff. Restrooms here.

22 miles from South Entrance · 30 min stop
10:15 AM

Old Faithful & Upper Geyser Basin

The icon. Watch her blow — up to 8,400 gallons of scalding water shooting 184 feet high. While you wait (or after), explore the Old Faithful Inn — the world's largest log structure, built in 1904. Walk the Upper Geyser Basin boardwalk to Morning Glory Pool (3-mile round trip, totally flat, absolutely worth it).

Grab a coffee at the general store. This is a good spot for breakfast if you didn't pack it.

17 miles from West Thumb · 1.5–2 hr stop
12:00 PM

Grand Prismatic Spring

The most photographed spot in the park and it earns it. A massive hot spring in rings of orange, yellow, and deep turquoise. Two options: walk the boardwalk at Midway Geyser Basin for up-close views, or hike the short Fairy Falls overlook trail (~1.5 miles round trip) for the jaw-dropping bird's-eye view. Do the overlook. It's the one.

8 miles from Old Faithful · 30–45 min stop
1:00 PM

Firehole Canyon Drive

A quick one-way scenic loop off the main road. Beautiful canyon views, hot springs, and a swimming hole at Firehole Swimming Area that's legitimately warm from geothermal activity. In August, a dip is tempting. Pull over at one of the picnic areas for lunch.

~5 min detour · 20–30 min
1:30 PM

Picnic Lunch

Find a pullout or picnic area along the Firehole River. Eat the sandwiches you packed. Breathe. You're inside a supervolcano and it's gorgeous.

30–45 min
2:15 PM

Norris Geyser Basin

The hottest and most volatile thermal area in the park. Walk the Porcelain Basin boardwalk to see Steamboat Geyser — the world's tallest active geyser. The landscape here looks otherworldly: bleached white ground, hissing vents, neon-colored pools.

23 miles from Firehole area · 30–45 min stop
3:00 PM

Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone

This is the other "holy shit" moment. The canyon drops 1,000 feet, painted in reds, oranges, and yellows (that's where the park gets its name). The Lower Falls are twice the height of Niagara. Must-hit viewpoints:

Artist Point — the classic postcard view. Lookout Point — dramatic look straight down. Uncle Tom's Trail — 328 steel steps down to the base of the falls (not required, but unforgettable if your legs are up for it).

21 min from Norris · 1–2 hr stop
5:00 PM

Hayden Valley

The drive south from Canyon back toward Lake takes you through Yellowstone's best wildlife corridor. Go slow. Bison herds, elk, and possibly bears or wolves. Bring binoculars and patience.

Continuous drive · stop at pullouts
5:30 PM

Yellowstone Lake

The largest high-altitude lake in North America. By now the crowds have thinned and the light is turning golden. Stop at the overlooks, skip a rock, take the group photo.

Connects back to West Thumb and the South Entrance
6:00 PM

Head Home

South through Grand Teton NP back to Jackson. The evening light on the Tetons during this drive is unreal. You'll be tired, happy, and probably asleep by 9.

~1.5 hr drive back to cabins · ETA ~7:30 PM

Boat Day

Jackson Lake at the foot of the Tetons. Rent a boat, find a cove, float, swim, and soak in the last full day together.
Sunday
30

Morning: Hit the Lake

After a massive Yellowstone day, Sunday is all about being on the water with zero agenda. Head to Signal Mountain Marina in Grand Teton National Park (~30 min from Jackson) and pick up a boat by 10 AM.

Cruise Jackson Lake with the Teton Range towering straight out of the water. Find a sandy cove on the west shore, anchor, swim (it's cold — mountain lake cold — but refreshing after yesterday), float, and just exist. Pack a cooler the night before with drinks, snacks, and sandwiches.

Deck Cruiser — fits 12
$200/hr or $980 for a full day. One boat, whole crew. Book early — August is peak. Call: (307) 543-2831 ext 250.
Two Pontoon Boats — 10 each
$150/hr each or $720/day each. More room to spread out. Caravan together or split up and explore.

Practical: Gas + tax extra on rental. Bring sunscreen, towels, layers (mountain lake = colder than you think), and a waterproof speaker. Back at the marina by 2–3 PM. Signal Mountain Lodge has a restaurant right at the marina if you need food after.

Practical Notes

Getting There

Fly into Jackson Hole Airport (JAC). Direct flights from SFO, LAX, DEN, DFW, ORD, JFK, and more in summer. Book early — August is peak season and flights fill fast.

Rental Cars

You'll want 2–3 cars for 12 people. Reserve at the airport well in advance. Essential for Yellowstone day and getting between the cabins and town. SUVs recommended.

August Weather

Days: 75–85°F (24–29°C) and sunny. Nights: can drop to 40–50°F (4–10°C) — bring layers. Afternoon thunderstorms are common. Pack a rain layer and sunscreen.

Park Passes

One pass covers both Grand Teton and Yellowstone: $35/vehicle for 7 days. Buy at the entrance or online at recreation.gov ahead of time.

Cell Service

Good in Jackson. Spotty in Grand Teton. Basically nonexistent in Yellowstone. Download offline maps, share your Yellowstone game plan the night before, and embrace being unreachable for a day.

What to Pack

Layers (mornings are cold), hiking shoes, sunscreen, sunglasses, reusable water bottle, camera with charged batteries, bear spray if hiking, and one cute outfit for Saturday night.

Altitude

Jackson sits at 6,200 ft; Yellowstone at 8,000+. Drink extra water, go easy on Day 1, and maybe skip the second margarita (or don't — it's a bachelorette).

Wildlife Safety

This is real wilderness. Stay 100 yards from bears and wolves, 25 yards from bison and elk. They look chill. They are not chill. Do not approach them for a selfie.
