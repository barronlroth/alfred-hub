Roll In & Settle

The Lower Loop

96 miles of the most concentrated geological chaos on Earth. You'll enter through the South Entrance (~1h 15m from Jackson), loop through the park's greatest hits, and be back by dinner. Here's the route, timed out:

6:30 AM
Leave the Cabins
Coffee in hand, snacks packed. Drive north through Grand Teton National Park — the Tetons will be lit gold on your left.
7:45 AM
Enter Yellowstone (South Entrance)
$35/vehicle or use a parks pass. Buy online at recreation.gov in advance to skip the line.
8:00 AM
West Thumb Geyser Basin
First stop, right inside the entrance. Boardwalk loop along Yellowstone Lake with colorful hot springs steaming into the water. 30–45 min.
9:30 AM
Old Faithful
Check the eruption prediction at the visitor center (erupts roughly every 90 min). Explore the Upper Geyser Basin boardwalks while you wait. When it goes off — you'll understand the hype.
11:00 AM
Grand Prismatic Spring
The photo. Walk the Midway Geyser Basin boardwalks first, then drive to the Fairy Falls Trailhead and hike the overlook trail (~1.6 mi round trip) for the aerial rainbow view. Absolutely unreal.
12:30 PM
Lunch
Old Faithful Inn has a dining room and grab-and-go. Or eat the snacks you packed (honestly, better move — lines can be long).
1:30 PM
Drive to Canyon Village
45-minute scenic drive north through the park interior.
2:15 PM
Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone
Artist Point — the most iconic viewpoint in the park. Then hike to the Brink of the Lower Falls (steep but short) for an up-close look at the 308-foot waterfall crashing below. 1–1.5 hrs total.
4:00 PM
Hayden Valley
Drive slowly south through Yellowstone's prime wildlife corridor. Bison herds are almost guaranteed. Elk, wolves, and bears are possible. Pull over at turnouts. Take your time.
5:00 PM
Head Home
Continue south past Yellowstone Lake, exit through the South Entrance. Back in Jackson by ~6:15 PM. Pizza and collapse at the cabins, or rally for dinner in town — dealer's choice.

Yellowstone Tips

  • Bring layers — mountain weather shifts fast, even in August
  • Sunscreen, water bottles, and SNACKS. Food options in the park are limited.
  • Stay on boardwalks at thermal features. The ground is literally boiling underneath.
  • Cell service is spotty to nonexistent — download Google Maps offline before you go
  • Wildlife rules: 100 yards from bears/wolves, 25 yards from bison/elk. They are not pets.
  • Gas up before entering the park
  • Bear spray is recommended and available at park stores

Practical Notes

Getting There

Fly into Jackson Hole Airport (JAC). It's inside Grand Teton National Park — 15 min to town. Direct flights from most major cities in summer. Book early, August fills fast.

Rental Cars

You'll need 3 SUVs for 12 people (plus gear). Reserve well ahead — Jackson Hole rentals sell out. All the driving (Yellowstone, horseback, town) requires cars.

August Weather

Days: 75–85°F, sunny. Evenings: drops to 40–50°F. Afternoon thunderstorms are possible. Bring layers, a light jacket, and sunscreen. Yellowstone will be cooler at elevation.

What to Pack

Hiking shoes (not just sandals), layers for mountain weather, swimsuit for the hot tub and lake, something cute for town night, sunscreen, water bottle, portable charger.

Yellowstone Pass

$35 per vehicle at the gate, or buy an annual America the Beautiful pass ($80) at recreation.gov. Buy online to skip the entrance line. Covers Grand Teton too.

Cell Service

Good in Jackson. Spotty to nonexistent in Yellowstone and on remote roads. Download offline maps (Google Maps or Maps.me) before the Yellowstone day.

Groceries

Stock up on arrival at Jackson Whole Grocer (upscale) or Albertsons (normal). Get breakfast stuff, snacks, drinks. The cabins have a kitchen — use it for breakfasts to save time and money.

Bear Aware

This is bear country (grizzly and black bear). Carry bear spray in Yellowstone (sold at park stores). Keep food secured. Make noise on trails. They're more afraid of you, but let's keep it that way.
