The Algarve

Golden sea cliffs, hidden grottoes, and the best seafood in Europe — before anyone else shows up for summer.

Getting to the Algarve

Your Iberia flight lands in Madrid at 4:15 AM on May 14. Catch a morning connection to Faro (FAO) — Ryanair, TAP, and Iberia all fly it. Flight is ~1.5 hours, fares typically $30–50/person. You’ll be on Algarve soil by lunchtime. Rent a car at Faro Airport for the drive to Lagos (~1 hour west along the coast).

Wednesday
14

Arrive & Decompress

You’ve been travelling since yesterday evening. Check into your hotel in Lagos, shower off the plane, and ease into Portugal mode. Walk into the Old Town — cobblestoned streets, whitewashed buildings, bougainvillea everywhere. Find a terrace, order a cold imperial (that’s what they call a draught beer here), and let the jet lag wash away in the sun.

For dinner, keep it simple and local. Casinha do Petisco does excellent petiscos (Portuguese tapas) — or walk to the marina and pick a waterfront spot. You’re on holiday time now.

Thursday
15

Ponta da Piedade & Beach Day

Morning: walk to Ponta da Piedade, the Algarve’s crown jewel. Towering golden limestone stacks, arches, and sea caves — all within a 15-minute stroll from Lagos. Descend the wooden staircase to the water and book a boat or kayak tour through the grottoes. The light inside the caves is otherworldly.

Afternoon: pick your beach. Praia do Camilo (intimate cove, 200 stairs down, spectacular) or Praia da Dona Ana (easier access, consistently rated one of Europe’s best). May means warm enough to swim, empty enough to breathe.

Ponta da Piedade Boat Tour
~1 hour through arches and grottoes. ~€20/person from Lagos marina. Morning light is best.
Kayak Tour
Paddle into the caves yourselves. More intimate, more adventurous. Book a guided 2-hr tour ~€35/person.
Friday
16

Benagil Cave & Seven Hanging Valleys

Day trip east along the coast (~30 min drive). Morning: Benagil Cave — that cathedral-like sea cave with the hole in the ceiling you’ve seen on every travel Instagram. Access by boat tour or SUP from Benagil beach. Go early before the tour boats stack up.

Afternoon: the Seven Hanging Valleys Trail (Percurso dos Sete Vales Suspensos). A 6km cliff-top walk from Praia da Marinha to Benagil with jaw-dropping views the entire way — sea stacks, sinkholes, hidden beaches below. Widely considered the best hike in the Algarve. Bring water and good shoes.

Evening: dinner in Carvoeiro on the way back — charming small town built into a cliff cove.

Saturday
17

Sagres & the End of the World

Drive 30 minutes west to Sagres — the windswept southwestern tip of Europe. The Portuguese called it o fim do mundo (the end of the world), and standing on the cliffs of Cabo de São Vicente, you’ll understand why. This is where Prince Henry the Navigator launched the Age of Discovery.

Explore the Fortaleza de Sagres (clifftop fortress), watch surfers tackle the Atlantic breaks, and grab lunch at a clifftop restaurant. A Tasca in Sagres is excellent — fresh-caught fish, no fuss.

Back in Lagos for a lazy afternoon. Sunset drinks at Bon Vivant (rooftop bar overlooking the marina) or along the waterfront.

Slow Day — Lagos & Tavira

Morning: sleep in, wander the Lagos farmers market (Mercado Municipal), grab pastéis de nata from a bakery, and just soak in the town you’ve been too busy adventuring to fully explore.

Or, if you want one more adventure: drive east to Tavira (~1 hour). The most romantic town in the Algarve — Renaissance churches, a Roman bridge, castle ruins, and island beaches accessible by boat. Famous for its €1 oysters at the market. Worth it.

This is also the day to decide how you’re getting to Porto tomorrow — see the options below.

Douro Valley

Terraced vineyards, river cruises, and port wine — with Zach & Lauren along for the ride.

The Crew Assembles

Pick up Zach & Lauren at Porto Airport (OPO) at 10 AM. Grab the rental car and drive ~1.5 hours east into the Douro Valley. Winding roads through increasingly dramatic terraced hillsides. Stop for lunch along the way — there are excellent riverside spots around Peso da Régua.

Arrive at Octant Douro

Check in to Octant Douro in Castelo de Paiva. Settle into the rooms, explore the property, and ease into wine country pace. The hotel sits above the river with sweeping valley views — the kind of place where you immediately stop caring what time it is.

Evening: dinner at the hotel restaurant or explore nearby. The property has its own restaurant with local cuisine and an excellent wine list. After two planes and a drive, let someone else cook.

Wine, River & Sunset Cruise

Morning: visit a quinta (wine estate) for a tasting and tour. The Douro is Portugal’s most famous wine region — port wine, obviously, but also stunning reds and whites. Quinta da Pacheca and Quinta do Crasto are both excellent and within driving distance.

Afternoon: recharge at the hotel pool.

Evening: the highlight — your private Rabelo boat cruise at 6 PM. Two hours on a traditional flat-bottomed wine boat with cocktails, wine, beer, and a Bluetooth speaker. The sunset over the terraced valley from the river is genuinely one of the most beautiful things in Portugal.

Lisbon

The city of seven hills, azulejo tiles, and pastéis de nata — plus a wedding.

Getting to Lisbon

Say goodbye to Zach & Lauren at checkout on May 22 (they head back from Porto). Drive from the Douro to Lisbon — ~3.5–4 hours via the A1 motorway. Drop the rental car in Lisbon (you won’t need it in the city — everything is walkable, tram, or cheap Uber).

Thursday
22

Arrive & Explore

Check in to Torel Palace and get your bearings. The hotel is in a gorgeous 19th-century palace in the Jardim Torel neighbourhood — central, elevated, with city views from the garden.

Afternoon: wander Alfama — Lisbon’s oldest district. A labyrinth of narrow alleys, tiled facades, and unexpected viewpoints. Get deliberately lost. End up at Miradouro da Graça or Miradouro da Senhora do Monte for sunset over the city.

Dinner: JNcQUOI Avenida on Av. da Liberdade — upscale Portuguese with a beautiful interior. Alfred is monitoring booking availability and will auto-reserve when it opens (~2 months out).

23

Belém, Nata & Nightlife

Morning: tram or Uber to Belém. Visit the Jerónimos Monastery (Manueline masterpiece, UNESCO World Heritage), the Tower of Belém, and — most importantly — Pastéis de Belém for the original custard tart. Accept no substitutes.

Afternoon: back to the city centre. Browse the shops on Rua Augusta, ride Tram 28 through the hills (touristy but genuinely fun), or pop into the National Tile Museum (Museu do Azulejo) — because after two weeks of seeing those blue tiles everywhere, you’ll want to understand them.

Night: Bairro Alto. Lisbon’s nightlife district. Dozens of tiny bars crammed into narrow streets. Start with dinner, let the night evolve. Cervejaria Ramiro for legendary seafood if you haven’t had your fill yet (spoiler: you haven’t).

Practical Notes

Flights

Out: IB 326, SFO → Madrid, May 13, 8:40 PM. Then Madrid → Faro (book separately). Back: DL 273 Lisbon → JFK, then DL 679 JFK → SFO, May 25.

Rental Car

Pick up at Faro Airport (May 14). Keep through Algarve & Douro. Drop off in Lisbon (May 22). You won’t need a car in Lisbon — everything is walkable or a cheap Uber.

May Weather

Algarve: 20–25°C (68–77°F), sunny, low chance of rain. Douro: similar, slightly cooler evenings in the valley. Lisbon: 18–24°C (64–75°F), pleasant. Pack layers for evenings.

Currency & Cards

Euro (€). Cards accepted almost everywhere. ATMs (Multibanco) are reliable. Avoid airport exchange — terrible rates. Some small restaurants are cash-preferred.

Language

Portuguese. English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learn “obrigado/obrigada” (thank you) and “uma imperial, por favor” (a beer, please) and you’re set.

Tolls

Portuguese highways (autoestradas) have electronic tolls. Rent a Via Verde transponder with the car or register your plate online. Do NOT skip this — fines are annoying.

Power

Type F outlets (European two-pin round). Bring a universal adapter or buy one at the airport. Voltage is 230V.

SIM / Data

Buy a local SIM at Faro Airport (Vodafone or MEO, ~€15–20 for 10GB). Or check if your US carrier has a good EU roaming plan. You’ll want data for maps in the Algarve and Douro.
