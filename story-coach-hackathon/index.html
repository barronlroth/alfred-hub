The prompt, verbatim

Track 1


Build a dynamic, non-linear storytelling prototype where the narrative and media assets adapt in real-time.


Design a system that dynamically adjusts character arcs, plot continuity, and visual or audio elements in response to user actions or environmental inputs, moving entirely away from traditional, static storytelling.


Your solution: What does success look like?


Excellent: An excellent project achieves true computational storytelling by dynamically adapting the narrative structure itself in real-time, demonstrating sophisticated state-tracking and seamless, multi-modal responsiveness to user and environmental inputs.


Fair: A fair project creates an engaging interactive experience, but relies primarily on accelerating traditional pipelines, e.g. dynamically generating assets for a pre-set, branching path, rather than fundamentally altering the narrative structure in real-time.


Insufficient: A bad project fails to leverage generative AI for responsiveness, merely automating a standard, linear timeline or mimicking a traditional static decision-tree structure.

How we broke it down

The key distinction is not “more media.” It is “more story state.”

The prompt is not asking for a better content pipeline. It is asking for a system where user input changes the underlying narrative model: arcs, continuity, tension, causality, and future possibilities.

Fair

Generated branches

Beautiful assets layered onto a pre-set choice tree.

Excellent

Computational storytelling

A living story state that changes based on user behavior.

Risk

Static interactivity

Choose-your-own-adventure can look dynamic while remaining structurally fixed.

Target

Adaptive structure

Every input should affect what the story believes is true.

Media is evidence; state is the invention

Initial concept

Our first read was broad: adaptive video on demand.

A viewer watches a scene, makes a choice, and the system generates the next segment. It feels like choose-your-own-adventure, but with generative media instead of pre-filmed branches.

Viewer sees a scene.
A short generated video establishes the world.

Viewer chooses or speaks an action.
The next story beat responds to their input.

System updates continuity.
Character state, setting, and unresolved threads persist.

Model generates the next segment.
The media adapts, rather than selecting from a fixed file tree.

Two user groups

We were really looking at two audiences.

Viewers want agency. Creators want leverage. The first concept tried to serve both, which made the product powerful but fuzzy.

Viewer

From consumer to participant

They do not just watch; their decisions reshape the story.

Creator

From editor to world-builder

They define characters, rules, tone, constraints, and possible story physics.

Problem

Too horizontal

Entertainment, creator tools, video generation, and interactive narrative all at once.

Learning

Find a sharper wedge

The best demo needs a user with a concrete pain, not just a cool capability.

The pivot starts here
6
The pivot

Instead of “storytelling for everyone,” slice into parents and children.

What if the highest-value storytelling prototype is not entertainment, but safe creative development?

That reframes the project from an AI media generator into an answer to a real parent anxiety: whether AI helps children grow creatively, or quietly trains them to outsource imagination.

Narrower problem, stronger thesis
7
The parent problem

Parents have a trust gap with AI for kids.

Generative AI is so capable that it creates a new developmental question: if a child can instantly generate polished images and stories, will they still practice drawing, imagining, sequencing, struggling, and revising?

Safety.
Is this appropriate, bounded, and understandable?

Agency.
Is the child making creative decisions, or just prompting a machine?

Development.
Does AI scaffold growth, or stunt the skills it automates?

Imagination.
Will children still want to draw when image generation is effortless?

The emotional wedge

Story Coach

The child remains the author. The AI becomes the coach.

The app asks the child to draw each story beat. The AI interprets the drawing, preserves the child’s idea, updates story state, and asks the next question.

The core mechanic

Every drawing becomes canon.

Drawing as input

The child creates the raw imaginative artifact first.

AI as interpreter

The system maps the drawing to story function: character, goal, obstacle, helper, action, ending.

State as memory

The app remembers protagonist, traits, visual details, goals, obstacles, and continuity rules.

Prompt as pedagogy

The next prompt teaches story structure without lecturing.

The product loop
10
Why it fits Track 1

It turns creativity into a live narrative state machine.

The system does not merely generate a book. It tracks the child’s decisions and adapts future prompts, continuity, character development, and media based on what the child contributes.

Character arcs

Traits and emotional changes persist across pages.

Plot continuity

Goals, obstacles, objects, and visual features are remembered.

Media adaptation

Drawings become polished pages while preserving the child’s source idea.

Responsive prompts

The next question changes based on what the story still needs.

Stateful storytelling

12
Ages 4 to 6 as the first lesson

Start with early storytelling structure, then generalize.

For ages 4 to 6, the lesson is simple narrative grammar: character, trait, want, obstacle, action, ending. But the pattern could apply to any creative lesson that benefits from guided practice.

Storytelling

Build a beginning, middle, and end through drawing.

Visual expression

Use AI to extend the child’s drawing, not erase it.

Language

Prompt the child to describe what they made and why it matters.

Creative confidence

Reward effort, revision, and imagination over polished output.

Wedge now, platform later

Positioning

Our current answer to the prompt.

Story Coach is a co-creative storytelling engine, demonstrated through a children’s drawing app.

The child supplies imagination. The AI supplies structure, continuity, gentle feedback, and multimodal transformation. The result is not passive AI content. It is creative scaffolding.
