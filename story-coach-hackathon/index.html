Verbatim prompt

The brief

Track 1

Build a dynamic, non-linear storytelling prototype where the narrative and media assets adapt in real-time.

Design a system that dynamically adjusts character arcs, plot continuity, and visual or audio elements in response to user actions or environmental inputs, moving entirely away from traditional, static storytelling.

Start with the assignment, not the solution.
03
bar
Verbatim success criteria

The bar

Excellent

An excellent project achieves true computational storytelling by dynamically adapting the narrative structure itself in real-time, demonstrating sophisticated state-tracking and seamless, multi-modal responsiveness to user and environmental inputs.

Fair

A fair project creates an engaging interactive experience, but relies primarily on accelerating traditional pipelines, e.g. dynamically generating assets for a pre-set, branching path, rather than fundamentally altering the narrative structure in real-time.

Insufficient

A bad project fails to leverage generative AI for responsiveness, merely automating a standard, linear timeline or mimicking a traditional static decision-tree structure.

The danger is a prettier branch tree.

Model stack

Nano Banana

Veo or Omni

New wedge

Parents first

The stronger question is not whether AI can make better stories. It is whether AI can help children become better storytellers.

Narrower audience. Better tension.

The fear

Safety

Is this bounded enough for kids?

Agency

Growth

Does AI scaffold the skill it automates?

Imagination

10
answer
Our answer

The child stays the author.

AI becomes the coach: it preserves the child’s drawing, names the story beat, keeps continuity, and asks the next useful question.

Co-creation beats replacement.
11
loop
Story Coach loop

Draw, then think

For ages 4 to 6, the lesson is basic story grammar.

Draw the character
Choose what they are like
Draw what they want
Draw the problem
Draw what they try
Draw the ending
The child learns structure without a lecture.
