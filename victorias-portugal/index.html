Jul 15 – 19 / 4 Nights

Ericeira

Europe's only World Surfing Reserve. A whitewashed fishing village on Atlantic cliffs, 45 minutes from Lisbon.
Wednesday
15

Arrival

Land in Lisbon, pick up a rental car at the airport, and drive 45 minutes north along the coast to Ericeira. Drop bags at the hotel, stroll the cobblestoned old town. Grilled sardines and a cold Sagres on a terrace above the Atlantic.

Thursday
16

First Surf Day

Morning beginner lesson at Foz do Lizandro — a wide sandy beach with gentle, forgiving waves. Multiple schools, full gear rental, patient instructors. Afternoon in town — backstreets, surf shops, açaí bowls. Dinner: arroz de marisco at a seaside table.

Foz do Lizandro
Wide sandy beach, perfect beginner waves. 15-min walk south of town.
Praia do Sul
Sheltered beach break right in town. Good on calmer days.
São Julião
Long beach break just south. Gentle whitewash, ideal for beginners.
Friday
17

Sintra Day Trip

Drive 30 minutes to Sintra — a UNESCO wonderland of pastel palaces in misty forests. Hit Pena Palace early, wander the Moorish Castle walls, descend into town for travesseiros from Piriquita.

Optional: detour to Cabo da Roca on the way back — continental Europe's westernmost point. Dramatic cliffs, wild wind, spectacular sunset.

Saturday
18

Surf & Wine

Morning: second surf session. Try a different beach or level up at Ribeira d'Ilhas, Ericeira's most famous break (works for beginners on smaller days).

Afternoon: drive 20 minutes inland to the Lisbon wine region. Rolling hills around Mafra and Torres Vedras produce excellent whites and light reds.

Adega Mãe
Modern winery near Torres Vedras. Great whites, beautiful tasting room.
Quinta de Sant'Ana
Family-run organic vineyard in Mafra. Charming, intimate, no crowds.

Where to stay

Immerso Hotel

Design-forward boutique on the cliffs above Ericeira. Clean lines, ocean views from every room, an infinity pool that melts into the Atlantic. Small enough to feel intimate, polished enough to feel special.

Jul 19 – 21 / 2 Nights

Évora

UNESCO World Heritage city in the heart of the Alentejo. Roman ruins, medieval walls, and Portugal's greatest wine region.
Sunday
19

Arrival & the Wedding

Drive ~2 hours southeast through golden Alentejo plains. Check in, explore the historic center on foot. The Roman Temple of Diana sits impossibly well-preserved in the center. The Chapel of Bones at the Church of São Francisco is eerie and unforgettable.

Evening: wedding festivities begin.

Monday
20

Wedding Day

Today belongs to the celebration. Évora's ancient streets make a stunning backdrop. If there's free time, climb the Cathedral rooftop terrace for panoramic views, or sit in Praça do Giraldo with a galão and people-watch.

Tuesday
21

Alentejo Wineries → Comporta

Morning at one of the Alentejo's legendary wine estates. This region produces Portugal's most celebrated reds — full-bodied, earthy, built for the landscape. Afternoon: drive ~1.5 hours west to Comporta.

Wineries
Winery & Restaurant
Herdade do Esporão

One of Portugal's most respected estates. Beautiful grounds, excellent tasting experiences, farm-to-table restaurant. Book ahead — ~40 min from Évora but worth every minute.

Historic Winery
Adega da Cartuxa

Producers of the legendary Pêra-Manca. Just outside Évora's walls — taste wines that have been made here for centuries.

Family Estate
Ervideira Wines

Family-run since the 1880s. Known for their Invisível sparkling rosé. 30 min south of Évora.

Where to stay

Albergaria do Calvário

A 16th-century olive oil mill converted into a boutique hotel inside Évora's ancient walls. Warm stone, vaulted ceilings, quiet gardens. Centuries of character, perfect mattress.

Jul 21 – 24 / 3 Nights

Comporta

Pine forests, rice paddies, and endless white sand. Portugal's barefoot-luxury coast — think Tulum without the crowds.
Tuesday
21

Arrival

Drive through the Alentejo countryside to Portugal's best-kept secret. Straw-roofed beach restaurants, pine forests stretching to white-sand beaches. Check in, walk to the nearest praia for a sunset swim. Dinner at a beach shack — fresh fish, local wine, sand between your toes.

Wednesday
22

Beach & Wine

Morning: rent bikes, ride through rice paddies to Praia da Comporta or Praia do Carvalhal. Miles of sand, almost no one around.

Afternoon wine tasting. The Setúbal peninsula produces fantastic moscatel and increasingly impressive reds.

Herdade da Comporta Wines
Estate wines from the local terroir. Tasting room in the village.
José Maria da Fonseca
Historic producer in nearby Azeitão (~40 min). Famous muscatel, beautiful cellar tour.
Thursday
23

Arrábida & Setúbal

Day trip to Serra da Arrábida — a lush natural park with hidden coves, turquoise water, and dramatic cliffs. Drive the winding coastal road, stop at Praia de Galapinhos (voted one of Europe's best beaches).

Continue to Setúbal for lunch. The Mercado do Livramento is one of Portugal's best food markets. Try choco frito (fried cuttlefish), the local specialty.

Friday
24

Last Beach Morning → Lisbon

Sunrise swim, long breakfast, reluctant checkout. Drive ~1.5 hours north across the Tagus bridge into Lisbon for the grand finale.

Where to stay

Sublime Comporta

Five-star retreat hidden in 17 hectares of pine and cork forest. Just 14 rooms — each a private world of warm wood, natural textiles, and floor-to-ceiling glass. Pool, spa, two restaurants. Barefoot luxury that actually delivers.

Comporta 5-star 14 rooms 2 restaurants
Also consider: Spatia Comporta (adults-only, 5-star, expanded 2025), Vermelho Melides by Christian Louboutin (bold, red, extraordinary)
~1.5 hr drive

Jul 24 – 26 / 2 Nights

Lisbon

The city of seven hills. Fado in narrow alleys, terracotta rooftops, and the best pastry on earth.
Friday
24

Arrive & Alfama

Check into a boutique hotel in the historic center. Start in Alfama — the oldest neighbourhood. A labyrinth of narrow streets, fado drifting from open windows, miradouros at every turn.

Hit Miradouro da Graça or Portas do Sol for golden hour over the rooftops. Dinner in Alfama or Chiado.

Saturday
25

Lisbon All Day

Morning: Belém — Jerónimos Monastery, Torre de Belém, and a mandatory pastel de nata at Pastéis de Belém (the original, since 1837).

Afternoon: LX Factory for industrial-chic browsing, Time Out Market for grazing, or vintage shopping in Príncipe Real.

Evening: ride Tram 28 at dusk. Final dinner at Cervejaria Ramiro for the definitive seafood feast — lobster, tiger prawns, percebes, and the legendary prego steak sandwich to close.

Pastéis de Belém
The original 1837 pastéis de nata. Non-negotiable.
Cervejaria Ramiro
Legendary seafood hall. Get the prego to finish.
A Baiuca
Tiny fado restaurant in Alfama. Intimate, authentic, unforgettable.

Departure

One last Portuguese coffee, drop the rental car at the airport, and fly home with sand still in your shoes and wine on your breath. Até logo.

Where to stay

Memmo Alfama

Perched in the heart of Alfama with panoramic terrace views over the Tagus. Sleek rooms, rooftop pool, steps from Lisbon's most atmospheric streets. Boutique without being precious.

Alfama, Lisbon Boutique Rooftop pool
Also consider: Verride Palácio (18th-century palace, insane views), The Lumiares (Bairro Alto, converted palace)

Practical Notes

Rental Car

Pick up at Lisbon Airport on arrival, drop off on departure. Essential for this route — all drives are scenic and under 2 hours. Book through Discovercars or Auto Jardim.

Connectivity

EU roaming works for most plans. Otherwise grab a Vodafone or MEO prepaid SIM at the airport. WiFi is excellent everywhere.

Money

Euro. Cards accepted almost everywhere. Bring cash for small beach bars, tips, and market stalls. Tipping 5–10% appreciated, not required.

July Weather

25–35°C. The coast stays cooler with Atlantic breezes. Évora will be the hottest stop — plan indoor activities midday. Pack sunscreen, hat, light layers for evenings.

Timing

Portugal is WET (UTC+1 in summer). Dinner is late — restaurants fill 8–9:30 PM. Embrace the rhythm.

Wine Notes

Alentejo reds are the stars. Ericeira's region does great whites. Comporta's moscatel is a must. Vinho verde from the north is everywhere and perfect for hot days.
